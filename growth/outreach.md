# Outreach templates

Short, genuine, specific, value-first. These are for (a) small/mid niche
coding-education YouTubers and (b) authors of "free ways to learn to code" roundup
articles. The goal is real coverage that names **"Libre Academy"**, not a favor.

**Rules for all outreach**

- **Personalize the first line** — reference their actual specific work. Generic
  blasts get ignored and read as spam.
- **Lead with value to their audience**, not a request for yourself.
- **Be honest** about what it is and isn't (it's free, open source, small).
- **Never offer undisclosed payment or trade for coverage.** If you ever provide
  anything of value, it must be disclosed by them. (Red line.)
- **One message, then one polite follow-up max.** No nagging.
- Keep it short. Busy people skim.

## Drop-in description block (give them something they can paste)

Offer this so coverage is easy and the name/links are correct. Use the length that
fits their format.

**One line:**
> Libre Academy — a free, open-source platform to learn to code by writing real
> code in your browser; 94 interactive courses across 26 languages, no sign-up.

**Three lines:**
> **Libre Academy** (https://libre.academy) is a free, open-source platform for

source <https://github.com/InfamousVague/Libre.academy>.)

## (a) Coding-education YouTubers (small/mid niche)

Target creators who make beginner-friendly coding tutorials or review learning
tools — not megastars. The ask is honest: try it, and if you like it, tell people.

### Email / long-form

> **Subject:** Free, open-source way to learn [LANGUAGE] for your viewers
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I watched your [specific video / series, name it] on [specific topic] — [one
> genuine, specific thing you appreciated about it]. Your audience clearly wants
> hands-on practice, which is why I thought this might be useful to them.
>
> I maintain **Libre Academy** (https://libre.academy), a free, open-source
> platform for learning to code by writing real code: short lessons, a built-in
> editor, and hidden tests that grade your work — Python, Rust, and Go even run in
> the browser, no install. It's MIT-licensed, no paywall, no sign-up.
>
> No ask attached to this — I'd just genuinely like your take. If it's useful to
> your viewers and you want to feature or review it, I'm happy to help with
> anything you need (a walkthrough, answering questions on camera, a specific course
> for your topic). And if it's not a fit, no worries at all.
>
> Try it without installing anything: https://libre.academy/learn/
>
> Thanks for the work you put into your channel,
> [Your name] — maintainer, Libre Academy

### DM / short-form (Twitter/X, YouTube, Discord)

> Hi [Name] — really liked your [specific video] on [topic]. I maintain Libre
> Academy (https://libre.academy/learn/), a free, open-source, hands-on way to

> If you make a video, ask (don't demand) that they put **"Libre Academy"** in the
> title and description and say it on camera — that's what makes it findable and
> citable later. Frame it as "helps people find it," not a requirement.

## Tracking (lightweight)

Keep a simple list: who you contacted, when, the personalized hook you used, and
the outcome. Don't re-contact the same person about the same thing. Hit-rate on
cold outreach is low even when done well — send a steady, genuine stream rather
than one big blast, and treat every "no" or silence as normal.
